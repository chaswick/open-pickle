Groups are the basic container in the app.

  • Global Competition: Start from Home → Global Competition → Start Session or Join Session during the current 6-week season.
  • Sessions: A session is a temporary group for the people you are playing with right now.
  • Sessions expire automatically: They are meant for quick competition play, not permanent group management.
  • Groups and Sessions use Invites: Open the session or group and share its invite code, invite link, or QR code to pull players into that roster.
  • Private leagues: Use these for long-running invite-only play. They have their own seasons, standings, and round robins.
  • Create a private league: Home → Private Leagues → Create a League.
  • Invite others: Open the session or private league and (admins only) use the Invite Code or Session Info card.
  • Enable invites: Tap Enable Invite to generate an invite.
  • Share: Read the invite code out loud, show the QR code, or tap Copy link.
  • Lock it back down: Use Disable Invite if you want to stop new joins.
  • Join either one: Enter the invite code in the app, scan the invite QR, or open the invite link sent to you by an admin.
  • Where to switch: Use Home → Global Competition to get into a session, then open that session to record global competition results. Go Home → Private Leagues for your private leagues.

Short version: a session is still a group, just a temporary one built for the current global competition season.

Play Plans helps your private league coordinate pickup games and see who’s in.

  • Create a plan: Open your private league, then go to Play Plans, pick Date and Time, then tap Add.
  • RSVP: For each plan, tap In, Maybe, or No. You can change your RSVP later.
  • Notifications: On the Play Plans page you can opt into email notifications and enable push notifications (recommended if you install the app to your home screen).

Play Plans are for long-running private leagues, not temporary competition sessions. Times are shown in your selected time zone (defaults to Eastern Time).

Check In lets you share where you are playing and see how many other players are checked in there.

  • You start it: We only ask for your location after you tap the Check In button.
  • Permission is explicit: Because Check In runs through the browser/PWA, your location is blocked until you allow it. We do not collect background location for Check In.
  • PWA tip: Check In usually works best after installing the app to your home screen and allowing location access there.
  • Known spots are fast: If you are at one of your saved locations, the app checks you in there right away.
  • Nearby names are flexible: If other players already use that same place with slightly different names, you can pick from suggestions or enter your own name.
  • It expires automatically: Each check-in stays active for 3 hours, then clears on its own.

Check In uses your current location only when you request it, and it works best over HTTPS or from the installed home-screen app.

Manual Logging: Prefer the classic form? Use the pencil option from the Log Match page. Both methods create the same match record and update standings.

Guests: If someone doesn’t have an account (or you don’t want to select them), choose Guest for that player slot.

  • Guests never appear in Standings and do not receive points / rating changes.
  • Matches with guests still count for the real players, but the same score-margin curve is scaled down for fairness: 1 guest = 60%, 2 guests = 40%.
  • Edit later if they join: You can log a match with a guest today, then edit that match later and replace the guest with their user account after they join.
  • Personal records (optional): Some casual groups allow guest-only opponent matches for tracking/trophies with 0 standings impact.
  • Guests never get rating changes, so only the registered players on the match receive the scaled result.

Voice Logging: Speak naturally to log matches hands-free. Try: “Pam and I beat Ryan and Amy 11-8.”

  • Smart Learning: When you correct voice recognition errors, the system learns from your edits to improve accuracy next time.
  • Player Names: Use nicknames or real names; the system recognizes both. Update your saved names in Account Settings.
  • Data Collection: The app does not send raw audio to our servers. Your browser's speech-recognition feature converts speech to text, which may use your device or browser provider. We send only the transcript to our server to interpret the match.

View & Export: Use “View All Matches” from the group standings area to see season history, or visit your user page to download your match history.

Standard Mode: When a match is logged, the opposing team receives a confirmation request. Only one member of the opposing team needs to confirm.

  • Confirm: Accept the result as logged.
  • Edit: The side currently responsible for confirmation can edit a provisional match. Editing keeps the editor’s side attested and makes every other unrepresented side review again.
  • Dispute: Use this on a provisional match you should not confirm, such as a match you never played.
  • 48-hour window: In Standard Mode, if the match is still unconfirmed after 48 hours, it is nullified and will not count in standings.
  • No Cherry Picking: Selectively confirming only wins can trigger visible season warnings and eventually remove you from competition matches for the rest of that season.

Casual Mode: In trusted groups, match loggers can immediately confirm their own entries after reviewing for errors.

Admins can change the confirmation mode in League Config.

Confirmed-match removal is the cleanup path for results both teams already confirmed but later realize should not stay in the season.

  • When to use it: Duplicates, accidental confirmations, or a result both teams agree should be removed.
  • How it starts: Any participant on either team can request removal from the match row in recent/history views.
  • How it finishes: One participant from the other team must approve within 48 hours. Then the match is nullified and removed from standings and rating calculations.
  • If the other team ignores it: The request expires and the match stays.
  • How abuse is limited: Nobody can erase a confirmed loss alone. It is still team-based, but it requires agreement from both sides.
  • History stays visible: Nullified matches can still appear in match history with a Nullified label so the cleanup is not hidden.

Use Edit for a provisional match that only needs correction. Use Dispute for a provisional match you should not confirm. Use confirmed-match removal only after both sides already confirmed and later agree it should come out.

Rankings use a simple score-margin curve:

  • 1000 baseline: Ratings display from a familiar 1000 starting point.
  • Dominance, not format: A shutout like 9-0 or 11-0 moves rating the most.
  • Closer scores move less: The curve looks at how one-sided the game was across the total points played, so 11-9, 12-10, and 22-20 are all small swings compared with 11-2.
  • Recent variety and streaks matter too: After the margin sets the base rating change, the last 5 matches can boost winning results when you mix with more unique real partners and opponents. Reusing the same small pod in different partner/opponent roles still counts for some variety. Guest seats still reduce the bonus, and win-streaks award bonuses for 3, 4, and 5+ straight wins.
  • Guest Matches: Guests don’t earn points, and standings-impact matches with guests are weighted less (1 guest = 60%, 2 guests = 40%). If a guest later joins, you can edit the match and replace that guest with their real account. Personal-record matches (guest-only opponents) do not affect standings.

Confirmed results replay through the season so standings stay consistent.

What are Round Robins? A structured format where every team plays every other team exactly once.

  • Private league setup: Open your private league, then go to Round Robins to create one.
  • Smart Pairing: Matches and court numbers are generated automatically.
  • Track Progress: See which matches are complete and which are pending.
  • Counts Toward League Standings: Round robin matches count toward that private league’s rankings and stats.

Great for events and organized play days inside a private league.

The system encourages clean, honest match results:

  • Log the score and your opponents have 48 hours to confirm it.
  • Confirm or dispute promptly: Only confirmed results count. If a provisional match is wrong, update it. Your opponents will have the opportunity to confirm your changes. If a provisional match is bogus, dispute it. If a confirmed result should come out entirely, use confirmed-match removal and let the other team approve it.
  • Global Competition has season enforcement: Repeated missed confirmations can trigger visible warnings and eventually lock you out of competition matches until the next season. Logging multiple disputed matches may have the same consequence.

Ratings are based on what happened on court. Fair-play warnings and lockouts are a separate system and currently apply to the public competition season, not private leagues.

The app uses AI in limited ways, with human oversight.

  • Development support: AI is used to help develop parts of the codebase, but that work is reviewed and directed by an experienced web developer.
  • Trophy art: Some trophy artwork has been generated with AI. Donations help fund commissioned replacements by human artists over time.
  • Not used on your app data: AI is not used to process your personal information, match data, check-ins, or other user-submitted data inside the app.

The goal is to use AI as a supervised creation tool to improve development speed and quality.

Current hard limits in the app:

  • League size: Up to 40 active members per private league.
  • Season starts: A private league can start at most 1 new season every 24 hours.
  • Leagues per user: Most accounts can create up to 3 private leagues.
  • Global Competition fair-play thresholds: First warning at 3 missed required confirmations in one season, final warning at 5, and competition lockout at 7.

These limits may change over time as the app evolves.

Global Competition is not trying to replace DUPR or Elo. Those systems are built to estimate long-term player skill. This is built to measure something different: who is performing best over a defined stretch of time against the people they actually play.

  • Recent performance, not abstract skill: It reflects how well you are playing right now, not just your underlying level over months or years.
  • Built around real play patterns: It compares results from your normal sessions, groups, and opponents.
  • Bad-faith gaming is limited: Like any system, honest logging matters. But the system is designed to be resilient: you choose who you play, only confirmed results count, bogus provisional logs can be disputed, confirmed mistakes can be removed only if both teams agree, repeated missed confirmations in Global Competition can trigger season warnings and lockout, and the standings model rewards broader recent variety.

Think of it as a season-long performance race, not a universal skill rating.
